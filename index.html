About me

I was born and raised in Glenview Illinois. My parents were born in South Korea and moved to America before I was born so that makes me a Korean-American. I've visited Korea a couple times and I would say Seoul, South Korea is my favorite place in the world. I used to have hopes of teaching english there when I graduated from university but unfortunately, I am a dual citizen, so if I stay in Korea longer than 90 days, I would have to declare residency and that would mean I would be elligible to be drafted into the Korean army.
Pertaining to my education, I went to Marquette University and graduated with a mechanical engineering degree. The semester before I graduated, I took an intro to coding class and discovered how much enjoyment I got out of coding. So post graduation, when I stumbled upon an opportunity to apply for a coding bootcamp at Northwestern University, I couldn't let that chance go. I applied and got accepted. Now I'm pursuing a certificate in coding at Northwestern University and hope to combine both my degree in Engineering and my certificate in coding to create a fulfilling future for myself.
